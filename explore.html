RECORDING TEAM COMMITMENT STANDARDS

Commitment is not perfect attendance.
Commitment remains visible.

We understand work, school, family responsibilities, health concerns, and emergencies. What we require is consistent communication, spiritual connection, active participation, and responsibility toward schedules each member personally accepts.

APPLIES TO ACTIVE SINGERS & INSTRUMENTALISTS

01COMMUNICATION

Consistent Communication

Whenever availability, attendance, or confirmation is requested, every member must respond within the given deadline.

  • Valid replies: Available
  • Valid replies: Not available
  • Valid replies: Unsure, but will confirm
  • Valid reasons: Work, school, family, health, or emergency concern
The issue is not being unavailable. The issue is repeatedly failing to communicate.
Communicate clearly and within the deadline.
02ACCOUNTABILITY

Communication Warnings

Accountability follows a progressive and private process.

  • First consecutive failure: Formal warning or sanction
  • Second consecutive failure: Possible removal from active membership
  • Exception: A valid emergency or reasonable explanation will be considered
These steps are not meant to punish honest limitations. They protect reliability, fairness, and respect for the whole team.
Public standard. Private and fair handling.
03SPIRITUAL CONNECTION

Stay Connected Through the Lesson

Young Professional preaching or teaching sessions may be recorded and uploaded as an audio lesson or podcast.

  • Members who cannot attend must listen to the recording.
  • Submit a simple takeaway or personal application.
  • Remain connected to the spiritual direction of the ministry even when absent.
  • Consecutive failure may lead to warning, sanction, or membership review.
Absence should not disconnect a member from the message.
04MONTHLY

Participation & Evaluation

A monthly evaluation meeting may be conducted physically or virtually, depending on the availability of the majority.

  • Attendance and communication
  • Lesson completion
  • Recording progress
  • Concerns and suggestions
  • Scheduling and future plans
  • Evaluation of the current ministry system
Important team decisions may be discussed and settled through voting whenever appropriate.
Evaluation helps the system improve—not only the individual.
05RECORDING SESSION

Dedicated Morning Recording

Recording sessions may be scheduled separately in the morning, possibly beginning at 7:00 AM. They are not automatically held after the church service or on a weekday.

  • Two or three singers may be assigned based on availability.
  • Not every member must attend every recording day.
  • Only selected and confirmed members are expected to come.
  • Time is reserved for prayer, warm-up, rehearsal, corrections, and unhurried recording.
The goal is preparation, quality, and enough time to finish well.
06CONFIRMED SCHEDULE

Honor the Schedule You Accept

Once a singer or instrumentalist confirms an assigned recording schedule, that member is expected to prepare, arrive on time, and honor the commitment.

  • Being unavailable before confirmation is acceptable.
  • Communicate immediately when a real emergency happens.
  • Confirming and then disappearing without notice is not acceptable.
  • Review assigned songs and arrangements before the session.
A confirmed “yes” carries responsibility.
07ONE-ON-ONE

Commitment Conversation

Each member may be invited to a short one-on-one conversation before continuing as an active recording-team member.

  • Discuss present capacity and level of commitment.
  • Express work, school, family, health, or scheduling limitations.
  • Ask questions or raise concerns about the ministry system.
  • Confirm willingness to follow the team standards moving forward.
The conversation is meant to understand the member—not to demand perfect availability.
Clarity first, then active participation.
08CORE PRINCIPLE

What Commitment Means

Commitment does not mean that you are always available.

  • Communicate even when you cannot attend.
  • Stay spiritually connected through the lesson.
  • Participate in evaluation and team direction.
  • Honor the schedules you personally accept.
  • Take responsibility for your preparation and growth.
We do not measure commitment through perfect attendance alone. We look for communication, connection, participation, and responsibility.
Availability changes. Commitment should remain visible.
