TEAM EXPECTATIONS

Talent may help you begin.
Character helps you continue.

These standards protect the ministry, respect everyone’s time, and help members grow without ignoring the realities of young professional life.

Swipe through the standards →

01STANDARD

Consistent Communication

Respond within the requested deadline when availability, attendance, confirmation, or an important team concern is raised. Being unavailable is acceptable. Repeated silence is the concern.

Communicate clearly and early.
02STANDARD

Responsible Preparation

Listen to assigned materials, review arrangements, practice your part, and arrive prepared according to your current ability. We expect effort and progress, not instant perfection.

Preparation is part of service.
03STANDARD

Attendance & Availability

Work, school, health, family, and emergencies are understood. Members should communicate honestly, confirm availability, and avoid preventable last-minute absence.

Unavailable is valid. Uncommunicated is different.
04STANDARD

Stay Connected

When you miss a session, you may still be expected to review recordings, lessons, arrangements, or important updates so you remain connected to the team’s direction.

Missing a session should not mean losing the lesson.
05STANDARD

Teachable Character

Feedback, correction, evaluation, and rehearsal adjustments are part of development. Members should respond with humility, honesty, and willingness to improve.

Correction should produce growth, not conflict.
06STANDARD

Service Before Performance

The goal is not simply to receive a part, appear in a recording, or stand on stage. Every role exists to support worship and the mission of the Young Professional Service.

The role is a responsibility, not only an opportunity.
07ACCOUNTABILITY

Concerns will be addressed privately and fairly.

Repeated concerns involving communication, preparation, attendance, or conduct may lead to private guidance, warning, evaluation, temporary inactivity, or changes in active participation.

Public standards. Private cases. Respectful process.
